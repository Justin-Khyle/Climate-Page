Los Angeles has been home to some of the most extravagant minds since the city's construction. Recently, on January 7, 2025, LA experienced a record-breaking wildfire in Pacific Palisades, east of Malibu. The fire grew to 23,448 acres before it could be contained.

Projected wildfire conditions from February through May 2025 show regional variability. In Northern California, expected precipitation and snowpack will likely suppress fire spread. Meanwhile, Southern California has seen a drier-than-average wet season, leading to heightened fire risk, especially in the South Coast region.

Consequently, significant wildfire potential is anticipated to remain within normal thresholds in the north. However, the South Coast faces an elevated probability of large-fire potential due to drought and dry fuels.
